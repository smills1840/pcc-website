The Case for Polish

Why Commercial Facilities Choose Polished Concrete

Unlike coatings that sit on top of the slab and can peel, polished concrete works with your existing floor — densifying and hardening it from within. The result is a surface that only improves with age.

savings

Lower Long-Term Cost

No recoating, no stripping, no wax. Maintenance is just a dust mop and occasional auto-scrubber.

light_mode

Improved Light Reflectivity

A polished floor reflects ambient light, reducing the need for overhead lighting and lowering energy costs.

forklift

Heavy Traffic Ready

Handles forklifts, pallet jacks, and constant foot traffic without cracking, chipping, or hazing.

How It Works

The Multi-Stage Polishing Process

Each stage uses progressively finer diamond tooling to grind, hone, and polish the concrete surface to your specified sheen level.

01
construction

Grinding

Coarse metal-bond diamonds remove surface blemishes, old coatings, and high spots. Opens the concrete profile for densifier penetration.

16–30 Grit
02
science

Densifying

A lithium silicate densifier is applied to chemically harden the slab. This fills micro-pores and dramatically increases surface hardness and abrasion resistance.

Chemical Treatment
03
blur_on

Honing

Transition to resin-bond diamonds in progressively finer grits to remove scratches from previous steps and begin developing sheen and clarity.

50–400 Grit
04
lens

Polishing

Fine to ultra-fine resin diamonds bring the floor to its final sheen — from a matte industrial look all the way to a mirror-like high-gloss finish.

800–3000 Grit

Polished Concrete vs. Epoxy Coating

Both are excellent — but polished concrete is often the right long-term choice for high-traffic commercial environments.

Polished Concrete Epoxy Coating
Lifespan Lifetime (with maintenance) 5–10 years
Peeling / Delamination Risk None — part of the slab Possible over time
Maintenance Dust mop & auto-scrub Periodic recoating
VOC Emissions Zero Low to moderate
Forklift Traffic Excellent Good
Decorative Options Sheen levels, dyes, aggregates Flakes, metallics, colors
